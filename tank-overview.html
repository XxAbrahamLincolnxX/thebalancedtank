Tank Overview

A 20 gallon planted tank: equipment, plants, hardscape, and the fish, shrimp, and snails that live here.

Tank & equipment

Tank: Marina 20 gallon, no lid.

Light: Hygger 60W 36–42 inch LED. Schedule:

  • 7 AM–12 PM — main light on
  • 12 PM–4 PM — blackout
  • 4 PM–9 PM — moonlight

Filter: S20 slim, run at about half strength.

Plants

All plants are listed with scientific names. Epiphytes (Anubias, Java fern) are attached to driftwood; moss is on the rocks.

  • Anubias barteri — Nana and broad leaf (on driftwood)
  • Sagittaria subulata
  • Hottonia palustris
  • Vallisneria americana 'Natans' — Jungle val
  • Lilaeopsis brasiliensis
  • Leptochilus pteropus — Java fern (on driftwood)
  • Phyllanthus fluitans — Red root floaters
  • Taxiphyllum alternans 'Taiwan Moss' (on rocks)
  • Hygrophila corymbosa 'Siamensis'

Substrate & hardscape

  • Substrate: 1 inch Tropica aqua soil with 1 inch of black sand on top.
  • Driftwood: 1 piece — used for Anubias and Java fern.
  • Rocks: 2 — used for Taiwan moss.

Inhabitants

Who lives in the tank and the conditions they like (simplified for quick reference).

Neocaridina shrimp

Neocaridina davidi

Small freshwater shrimp; they graze on biofilm and leftover food and are peaceful with fish and snails. Color variants (e.g. cherry, blue, yellow) can interbreed. Best kept in a group.

Size
~3 cm
Temp
18–29 °C (64–84 °F)
pH
6.5–8.0
Zone
Bottom; colony

Otocinclus

Otocinclus spp.

Small algae-eating catfish. They do best in a mature tank with plenty of biofilm and algae; keep them in a small group. Very peaceful and safe with shrimp.

Size
~4 cm
Temp
22–26 °C (72–79 °F)
pH
6.0–7.5
Zone
Bottom; group

Kubotai green rasbora

Microdevario kubotai

Small, peaceful schooling fish with a green shimmer. They swim in the mid-water and prefer to be in a group. Good in planted tanks with gentle flow.

Size
~2–2.5 cm
Temp
22–28 °C (72–82 °F)
pH
6.0–7.5
Zone
Mid-water; schooling

Ramshorn snails

Planorbidae (e.g. Planorbella spp.)

Small snails that help clean up leftover food and soft algae. They reproduce readily, so population is kept in check by not overfeeding.

Role
Clean-up; algae/detritus
Zone
Glass, substrate, plants

How they interact

Plants take up nutrients (including nitrate), provide cover, and add oxygen. Shrimp and otos graze on biofilm and algae; rasboras use the mid-water and don’t bother shrimp when well fed. Snails help with leftover food and soft algae. The filter and regular water changes keep the nitrogen cycle stable so everyone stays healthy.
