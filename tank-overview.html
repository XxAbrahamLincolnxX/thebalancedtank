Setup Notes

Tank Overview

A 20 gallon planted tank: equipment, plants, hardscape, and the fish, shrimp, and snails that live here.

Tank & equipment

Core hardware and schedule.

20 gal

Tank: Marina 20 gallon, no lid.

Filter: S20 slim, run at about half strength.

Light: Hygger 60W 36–42 inch LED.

Schedule

  • 7 AM–12 PM main
  • 12 PM–4 PM blackout
  • 4 PM–9 PM moonlight

Plants

All plants are listed with scientific names. Epiphytes (Anubias, Java fern) are attached to driftwood; moss is on the rocks. Reference images below (Wikimedia Commons; add your own to images/ for Lilaeopsis and red root floaters if desired).

Substrate & hardscape

  • Substrate 1 inch Tropica aqua soil with 1 inch of black sand on top.
  • Hardscape Driftwood: 1 piece (Anubias / Java fern) Rocks: 2 (Taiwan moss)

Reference: Planorbidae (Ramshorn)

Ramshorn snails

Planorbidae (e.g. Planorbella spp.)

Small snails that help clean up leftover food and soft algae. They reproduce readily, so population is kept in check by not overfeeding.

Role
Clean-up; algae/detritus
Zone
Glass, substrate, plants

How they interact

Plants take up nutrients (including nitrate), provide cover, and add oxygen. Shrimp and otos graze on biofilm and algae; rasboras use the mid-water and don’t bother shrimp when well fed. Snails help with leftover food and soft algae. The filter and regular water changes keep the nitrogen cycle stable so everyone stays healthy.
